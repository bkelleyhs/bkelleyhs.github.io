Bishop Kelley is a Lasallian Catholic high school of 850 plus students, grades 9 - 12, located in the center of the Tulsa metropolitan area on a campus of 33 acres. Founded in 1960, the school is owned by the Catholic Diocese of Tulsa and is under the direction of the Christian Brothers. Bishop Kelley is a comprehensive secondary school with a curriculum that is primarily college preparatory. The school serves Catholic and non-Catholic families in Tulsa and surrounding communities who seek a Christian environment of concern, trust, and growth.

The mission of this school is to provide a quality, Catholic education founded on the teachings and values of Jesus Christ. Bishop Kelley High School strives to develop individual hearts and minds committed to the service of others by caring for the integrated needs of the whole person - spiritual, academic, emotional, physical, and social. Such an education is a cooperative endeavor available to each student regardless of creed, race, sex, or economic status.


ABOUT OUR SCHOOL
  • Director-Principal: Brother David Poos, FSC
  • Academic Principal: Alan Weyland
  • Faculty: 4 Brothers, 3 Sisters, 4 Priests, 76 Lay Members
  • In the past 15 years, BK has had 93 National Merit semi-finalists
  • The academic decathlon team has nine state championships and three second-place finishes in the past 12 years
  • Consistently, more than 93 percent of Kelley graduates attend college
  • Kelley students, through the school's Christian Service program, annually contribute more than 17,000 hours of community service to the metropolitan Tulsa area
  • Students may participate in 11 different competitive sports
  • Approximately 20 percent of Bishop Kelley students receive financial aid
  • We expect students to be responsible, moral, productive citizens, and active and giving members of the school community
  • We believe students thrive in a free and open atmosphere of respect, in which students educate one another through mutual understanding and tolerance
  • We affirm the value of intellectual commitment and academic excellence, and we encourage students to assume a personal responsibility for their education
